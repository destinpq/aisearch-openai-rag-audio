Bernoulli's Equation

Line 1: Chapter 15: Fluid Dynamics and Bernoulli's Principle

Line 2:

Line 3: Bernoulli's equation is a fundamental principle in fluid dynamics that describes

Line 4: the relationship between pressure, velocity, and elevation in a flowing fluid.

Line 5:

Line 6: The mathematical form of Bernoulli's equation is:

Line 7:

Line 8: P₁ + ½ρv₁² + ρgh₁ = P₂ + ½ρv₂² + ρgh₂ = constant

Line 9:

Line 10: Where:

Line 11: - P = pressure of the fluid

Line 12: - ρ = density of the fluid

Line 13: - v = velocity of the fluid

Line 14: - g = acceleration due to gravity

Line 15: - h = height above reference point

Line 16:

Line 17: This equation demonstrates the conservation of energy in fluid flow.

Line 18: Applications include aircraft wing design, venturi meters, and water flow systems.

Line 19:

Line 20: Daniel Bernoulli first formulated this principle in his work "Hydrodynamica" in 1738.
